Driving Renewable Energy Development in Central Queensland



01

A large-scale renewable energy project

Boulder Creek Wind Farm is a large-scale renewable energy project, set to deliver up to 372 MW of onshore wind power through the construction of approximately 60 wind turbines. Located 40 km southwest of Rockhampton and 5 km west of Mount Morgan, this project, developed by Aula Energy, plays a critical role in supporting Queensland’s transition to clean energy. To facilitate its successful progression from development into delivery, Bombheq Partners provided expert leadership and strategic guidance over a four-month period, seconding a Project Director to oversee key project management activities. The Project Development Director played a crucial role in preparing the project for execution, conducting a readiness review to assess the capability of the proposed Construction Asset Management Team. Bombheq Partners also led stakeholder engagement efforts by assisting Aula’s leadership team in preparing documentation for their joint venture partner, CS Energy. Further, the team performed schedule optimisation using Acumen Fuse software, ensuring constructability and verifying the project’s integrated schedule. Landholder obligations under lease agreements were also streamlined through a structured process led by Bombheq Partners, minimising potential delays and ensuring compliance before mobilisation.

Strategic Execution and Delivering Measurable Outcomes

Bombheq Partners’s involvement was instrumental in ensuring project milestones were met, with the successful passage of the Gate 3 hold point and the achievement of a Financial Investment Decision. The team facilitated the awarding of key contracts, securing agreements for Turbine Supply and Installation (TSI), Balance of Plant (BOP), Construction Management, and Grid Connection. These strategic procurement efforts provided commercial certainty, ensuring the project remained on track for execution. Regulatory approvals were also a key focus, with Bombheq Partners supporting Aula Energy in obtaining planning modifications from the Department of Planning, Industry and Environment (DPIE). Additionally, the team led the development and implementation of a 100-day plan to guide post-Notice to Proceed (NTP) activities, ensuring a structured approach to project mobilisation. Further collaboration with the BOP contractor resulted in an optimised program that reduced the overall project duration by two months, enhancing efficiency and cost-effectiveness. Through expert project leadership, risk management, and stakeholder engagement, Bombheq Partners successfully positioned the Boulder Creek Wind Farm for success, reinforcing its role in Australia’s renewable energy future. After project commencement, Bombheq Partners provided comprehensive risk assessment services, including the review and enhancement of the existing project risk register, facilitation of workshops with subject matter experts to identify and assess new risks, and the application of Quantitative Cost and Schedule Risk Analysis (QCSRA) to evaluate potential cost and time impacts. The result was a set of robust, accurate, and timely insights from Quantitative Risk Assessments (QRA), both for contingency planning and ongoing risk management throughout the project’s delivery phase.
